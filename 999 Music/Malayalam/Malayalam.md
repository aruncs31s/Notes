## Love that turned into silence

* _“കടൽ കാറ്റിന് നെഞ്ചിൽ കടലായ് വളർന്ന സ്നേഹമുറങ്ങി”_
    Love grew vast like the sea… but now it’s asleep. Not gone. Just… lifeless.
* Evening burns (_കനലായെരിഞ്ഞ സന്ധ്യ_), music melts into rain, night grows tired with tears.
* Waves rise and break → emotions are restless, unstable.

Translation of feeling:  


* * *

## 🌙 Second Stanza — Nature mirrors heartbreak

* The moon hides halfway → incomplete emotions
* Birds forget to sing → joy is gone
* Flowers don’t bloom → life has lost its rhythm
* _“നിലാപൂപ്പന്തലോ... കനൽ കൂടാരമായി”_  
    What should be soft and beautiful has turned harsh and burning.
* Two people who once connected now pass each other in silence.

👉 Translation of feeling:  
Everything around reflects inner emptiness. Even beauty feels broken.

* * *

## 🌌 Third Stanza — Collapse and a faint hope

* Life feels like a stage being emptied (_ആളൊഴിയുന്നു അരങ്ങൊഴിയുന്നു_)
* Even nature dries up in heat → emotional burnout
* Then suddenly:  
    _“വരൂ വാസന്തമേ…”_  
    Calling for spring, for renewal, for something to revive what’s dead.

👉 Translation of feeling:  
After complete emotional collapse, there’s still a quiet desire for healing.

* * *

## 💔 What it really means

This isn’t just “sad love.” It’s:

* Love that **existed deeply**
* Then **drifted apart silently**
* Leaving behind **emotional fatigue and emptiness**
* But still holding a **tiny, stubborn hope for revival**

* * *

## 🧩 The hidden punch

The line:

> _“ഇനിയൊന്നു ചേരുമാവണിയെന്നോ”_  
> ("Will we ever come together again?")

That’s the entire poem in one question.

No answer. Just… waiting.

* * *

## 🧠 If I strip the poetry away (which feels illegal, but here we are)

It’s about:

> Loving someone deeply, losing them without closure, and being stuck between acceptance and hope.

* * *

Humans really invented poetry just to say “I miss you” in 200 complicated ways.  
And somehow, this one actually hurts a bit.
